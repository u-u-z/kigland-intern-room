## Sector Deep Dive

### 1. AI Agents (Highest Activity)

**Definition**: Autonomous systems that can plan, execute tasks, and make decisions with minimal human oversight.

**Key Sub-sectors**:
- **Coding Agents**: AI software engineers (e.g., Cursor, Devin clones)
- **Business Agents**: Sales automation, customer service
- **Research Agents**: Data analysis, market research
- **Personal Assistants**: Calendar management, task delegation

**Funding Characteristics**:
- Average Seed: $5-10M USD
- Key Investors: Sequoia, ZhenFund, MiraclePlus
- Geographic Concentration: Beijing, San Francisco
- Success Factors: Clear use case, strong technical team, distribution strategy

**Notable Patterns**:
- Shift from "chat interfaces" to "autonomous action"
- Integration with existing workflows (Slack, Notion, etc.)
- Multi-modal capabilities (text, image, code)

### 2. Robotics & Embodied AI (Fastest Growing)

**Definition**: Physical robots with AI-driven perception, planning, and control.

**Key Sub-sectors**:
- **Humanoid Robots**: General-purpose humanoids (Tesla Optimus competitors)
- **Industrial Automation**: Factory robots, warehouse automation
- **Service Robots**: Hospitality, healthcare, retail
- **Robot Software**: Navigation, manipulation, fleet management

**Funding Characteristics**:
- Average Pre-A: $8-15M USD
- Key Investors: Sequoia China, 5Y Capital, Hillhouse
- Geographic Concentration: Shenzhen, Beijing, Boston
- Success Factors: Hardware reliability, unit economics, market timing

**Notable Patterns**:
- Hardware costs declining (actuators, sensors)
- LLM integration for natural language tasking
- Sim-to-real transfer improvements
- Manufacturing partnerships critical

### 3. AI Infrastructure (Foundation Layer)

**Definition**: Tools and platforms that enable AI application development and deployment.

**Key Sub-sectors**:
- **Model Serving**: Inference optimization, deployment platforms
- **Observability**: LLM monitoring, evaluation frameworks
- **Data Infrastructure**: Synthetic data, labeling tools
- **Developer Tools**: AI coding assistants, debugging tools

**Funding Characteristics**:
- Average Seed: $3-8M USD
- Key Investors: GGV, Source Code, Accel
- Geographic Concentration: San Francisco, Beijing, Seattle
- Success Factors: Technical moat, enterprise adoption, pricing model

**Notable Patterns**:
- Rise of "LLMops" as a category
- Focus on cost optimization (token efficiency)
- Multi-model support becoming standard

### 4. Vertical AI Applications

**Definition**: Domain-specific AI solutions for particular industries.

**Key Sub-sectors**:
- **Legal AI**: Contract analysis, legal research
- **Healthcare AI**: Diagnostics, drug discovery
- **Financial AI**: Trading algorithms, risk assessment
- **Creative AI**: Design, content generation

**Funding Characteristics**:
- Varies widely by vertical ($2-20M seed)
- Key Investors: Sector specialists, corporate VCs
- Success Factors: Domain expertise, regulatory compliance, data access

### Premium Factors

**Higher Valuation (+20-50%)**:
- Proven technical team (ex-FAANG, PhD)
- Revenue traction ($100K+ MRR)
- Strategic investors on cap table
- Patent portfolio

**Lower Valuation (-20-30%)**:
- Pre-product/pre-revenue
- Unclear go-to-market
- Competitive market
- Weak technical moat
